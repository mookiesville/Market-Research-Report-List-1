<p><p>The gastrointestinal (GI) stent market comprises several types, each serving distinct clinical needs. Biliary stents are used to relieve obstructions in the bile ducts, while esophageal stents address blockages in the esophagus, often due to cancer. Duodenal stents facilitate food passage in cases of duodenal obstruction. Pancreatic stents assist in draining pancreatic fluids, particularly in pancreatitis cases. Colonic stents are employed to manage colonic obstructions. Each stent type plays a crucial role in enhancing patient quality of life and managing gastrointestinal disorders.</p></p>
