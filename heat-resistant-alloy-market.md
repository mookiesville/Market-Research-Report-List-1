<p><p>The heat-resistant alloy market comprises three primary types: nickel-based, iron-based, and cobalt-based alloys. Nickel-based alloys are renowned for their exceptional strength and oxidation resistance at high temperatures, making them ideal for aerospace and turbine applications. Iron-based alloys are more cost-effective and offer good heat resistance, commonly used in industrial furnaces and automotive components. Cobalt-based alloys excel in extreme environments due to their robust mechanical properties and corrosion resistance, often utilized in medical devices and gas turbines. Each type serves distinct industrial needs and applications.</p></p>
