Nerve grafting replaces damaged segments with healthy tissue from elsewhere in the body, suitable for more extensive injuries. Nerve conduits are synthetic or biological tubes that guide nerve regeneration, often used when direct repair isn’t feasible. Other treatments may include electrical stimulation or advanced therapies, broadening the scope of options for nerve repair.</p></p>
