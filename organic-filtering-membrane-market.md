<p><p>The organic filtering membrane market includes three main types: microfiltration, ultrafiltration, and nanofiltration. Microfiltration utilizes membranes with larger pore sizes to remove particulates and bacteria, making it suitable for clarification processes. Ultrafiltration employs smaller pores to retain larger molecules and colloids, often used in water purification and dairy processing. Nanofiltration features even smaller pores, allowing selective ion removal and organic compound separation, ideal for treating wastewater and softening hard water. Together, they cater to various industrial and environmental applications.</p></p>
