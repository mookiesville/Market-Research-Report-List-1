<p><p>The basalt fiber market comprises three primary types: chopped fiber, twisted roving, and twistless roving. Chopped fiber is commonly used for reinforcing composites, providing strength and lightweight properties. Twisted roving consists of continuous basalt strands twisted together, enhancing structural integrity and durability in various applications. Twistless roving, on the other hand, maintains the alignment of strands without twisting, offering superior flexibility and strength. Each type serves unique purposes across industries like construction, automotive, and aerospace, leveraging basalt fiber's exceptional properties.</p></p>
