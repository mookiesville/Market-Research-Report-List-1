<p><p>Multi-factor authentication (MFA) technology enhances security by requiring multiple verification methods. The market can be categorized by the number of factors used. Two-factor authentication (2FA) involves two verification methods, typically something you know (like a password) and something you have (like a smartphone). Three-factor authentication adds a third element, such as a biometric scan. Four-factor and five-factor authentication include additional layers like behavioral analytics or geo-location. As threats evolve, a higher number of factors enhances protection against unauthorized access.</p></p>
