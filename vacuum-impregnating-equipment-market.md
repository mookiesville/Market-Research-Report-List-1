<p><p>The Vacuum Impregnating Equipment market is divided into two main types: wet and dry. Wet vacuum impregnation utilizes a liquid resin or adhesive under a vacuum to fill voids in porous materials, enhancing their strength and durability. In contrast, dry vacuum impregnation involves the application of a powder or solid resin without the addition of a liquid, allowing for effective sealing and moisture prevention. Both methods cater to different industrial needs, focusing on improving performance and protective properties of components.</p></p>
