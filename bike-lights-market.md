<p><p>The bike lights market is segmented into two main types: headlights and taillights. Headlights are designed to illuminate the path ahead, enhancing visibility for cyclists during night rides or low-light conditions, while also making them more visible to drivers. Taillights, on the other hand, are mounted at the rear to signal a cyclist's presence to vehicles behind them, often using flashing or steady light modes. Together, these components enhance safety, encouraging more people to cycle confidently in various conditions.</p></p>
